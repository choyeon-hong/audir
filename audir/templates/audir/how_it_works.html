How to Measure Accurately?

X-rays are the most accurate way to measure digit length because the hormones influence bone development.
X1 on the figure is the most accurate measurment of the bone.

X1 : from base of proximal phalanx to distal phalanx tip.

However, it is often impractical to obtain X-rays in real-world studies.
Instead, common alternatives include:

    Direct: from finger crease to tip (palm side and including soft tissue).

    X2: from base of proximal phalanx to tip (dorsal side and including soft tissue).

But,
these methods include soft tissue, which can reduce accuracy.

Solution

Our method detects hand joints by using Mediapipe hand landmarks model and excludes soft tissue. It measures true finger lengths â€” regardless of hand orientation (palm or dorsal).

It is much closer to the accurate measurement method and provides better consistency, reproducibility, accuracy and convenience without X-rays.

MediaPipe hand landmarks model identifies 21 hand-knuckle landmarks, as illustrated in the figure. Our solution measure the distances between landmark 5 to 8 for the index finger, and 13 to 16 for the ring finger.
