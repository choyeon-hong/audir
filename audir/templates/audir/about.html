What is

Digit Ratio ?

The digit ratio refers to the length ratio between the index finger (2D) and the ring finger (4D). This ratio is associated with prenatal exposure to sex hormones, especially testosterone and estrogen. It has been linked to physical, psychological, and behavioural traits.

Well-known findings show that:

  • Men typically have a lower digit ratio than women.
  • Men with lower digit ratios are more likely to be risk-taking, assertive, competitive, and may show higher levels of aggression.
  • Women with higher digit ratios are often associated with greater empathy, social sensitivity, and verbal fluency.

Additionally, it has also been studied in relation to athletic performance, decision-making, sexual orientation, and correlations to certain diseases and cancer.

Motivation

However, traditional measurement methods are manual and vary across studies, leading to inconsistencies and inefficiencies.

We addresses this challenge by using computer vision technology to automatically detect hand landmarks through the MediaPipe pre-trained model, which simplifies the digit ratio measurement process.

Hormonal effects influence bone development, so X-rays are the most accurate way to measure digit length. X1 on the figure is the most accurate measurment of the bone.

X1 : from base of proximal phalanx to distal phalanx tip.

However, it is often impractical to obtain X-rays in real-world studies.
Instead, common alternatives include:

    Direct: from finger crease to tip (palm side and including soft tissue).

    X2: from base of proximal phalanx to tip (dorsal side and including soft tissue).

But, these methods include soft tissue, which can reduce accuracy.

Solution

Our method detects hand joints by using Mediapipe hand landmarks model and excludes soft tissue. It measures true finger lengths — regardless of hand orientation (palm or dorsal).

It is much closer to the accurate measurement method and provides better consistency, reproducibility, accuracy and convenience without X-rays.
